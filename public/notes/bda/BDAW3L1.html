Responsibilities of a Data Analyst

  • Data Acquisition: Collecting, acquiring, and combining data from multiple sources and formats. Sometimes, this involves querying databases or obtaining external data sets.
  • Data Cleaning (Wrangling/Scrubbing): Filtering, sorting, and fixing errors, duplicates, or inconsistencies. This is often the most time-consuming part of the analyst’s job.
  • Descriptive Analytics: Applying basic statistics to extract first-layer insights, identify patterns, and spot trends.
  • Advanced Statistical Modeling: Using regression, predictive, or prescriptive modeling to answer deeper business questions, like forecasting revenue or finding core patterns ($y = mx + c$).
  • Presenting Insights: Creating dashboards, charts, and reports—telling a compelling story with the data.
  • Documentation: Recording each step and decision for reproducibility and team sharing.

Professor mentioned in class: Cleaning data (“data wrangling”) usually takes the longest and is absolutely essential before any analysis. Analysts often need to combine variables that are text-based, requiring natural language processing.

Practice Questions
1. What is data wrangling and why is it important?
Answer: Data wrangling is the process of cleaning and organizing raw data into a format suitable for analysis. It is important because accurate analysis requires reliable, error-free data.

2. Name two responsibilities of a data analyst related to presenting results.
Answer: Creating charts and dashboards; documenting the analytics process.
Key Takeaways
  • Data acquisition and cleaning are foundational analyst tasks.
  • Statistical modeling helps uncover deeper insights.
  • Clear documentation enables reproducibility.

Essential Tools for Data Analysts

Tool/Software Purpose/Use
Spreadsheets (Excel, Google Sheets) Storing, organizing, and performing basic analysis on structured data.
Statistical Analysis Software Conducting deeper analysis; examples include AML, Python libraries (PyLearn), HTO AI.
Data Visualization Tools Making charts, graphs, and dashboards for effective presentation (Power BI, Tableau, Matplotlib).
Programming Languages Automation, custom analysis, and advanced processing (Python, R, Java, C, MATLAB).
SQL and Database Skills Querying and extracting data from relational/non-relational databases.
Big Data Processing Handling large datasets with Hadoop, Hive, Spark, etc.

Functional Skillsets

  • Statistics: Strong grasp of statistics and probability, including validation and finding logical errors.
  • Analytical Ability: Ability to make sense of patterns and trends, check plausibility, and formulate/test hypotheses ($H_0, H_a$).
  • Problem-Solving: Each analytics project brings unique challenges; flexible and creative thinking is required.
  • Data Visualization: Sense of aesthetics and the science of presenting data effectively.

Professor mentioned in class: Data visualization is both an art and a science. A compelling story emerges only when visuals are clear and engaging.

Hinglish summary:
Fundamental skills mein statistics, analytical soch, problem-solving aur data visualisation important hain. Aapko samajhna hoga ki data ke patterns kya keh rahe hain aur unko real world se connect karna hoga. Visualization toh bilkul creative kaam hai!
Practice Questions
1. Why is statistics important for data analysts?
Answer: It enables them to validate data, find errors, and interpret results correctly.

2. What is the role of analytical ability in a data analytics project?
Answer: It helps connect data patterns to real-world outcomes and make accurate hypotheses.
Key Takeaways
  • Statistics are the backbone of analytic work.
  • Analytical and problem-solving skills lead to better understanding and forecasting.
  • Good data visualization requires both aesthetic sense and scientific approach.

Soft Skills

  • Curiosity & Tenacity: Constantly asking questions and never giving up when encountering messy or confusing data.
  • Detail Orientation: Spotting errors, inconsistencies, and subtle patterns by scrutinizing data closely.
  • Passion for Data & Tech: Embracing numbers, technology, and continual self-improvement.
  • Documentation & Presentation: Recording work thoroughly, presenting findings in a compelling manner.
  • Project Management: Managing processes, timelines, and teams with clear planning.
  • Business Acumen: Understanding the business context to deliver insight that drives decisions.
