1. Introduction to Case Studies

In this lecture, we explore two real-world case studies that demonstrate how raw data can be transformed into compelling visual stories using AI-powered tools. These case studies showcase the practical application of Jasper (AI writing tool) and Tome (AI presentation tool) in different business contexts.

Key Learning Objective: Understanding how to convert sales data and clinical data into investor-oriented and policy-maker-oriented presentations respectively. The focus is on changing perspectives from technical data to human-centered storytelling.
The professor emphasized that these are real-world scenarios where companies actually faced challenges in communicating their data effectively. 2. Case Study 1: Retail Analytics - Turning Sales Data into an Investor Story

2.1 Context & Challenge

Company: UrbanThreads
Industry: E-commerce Fashion Brand (Mid-sized Indian company)
Problem Statement: 40% sales growth achieved but unable to explain WHY and HOW to sustain it

The Business Situation

UrbanThreads, a mid-sized Indian e-commerce fashion brand, experienced significant growth over 12 months. While the 40% increase in sales was impressive, the management team faced a critical challenge: they couldn't effectively communicate the reasons behind this growth or their strategy for sustaining it to potential investors.

What Management Needed

The management wanted to prepare an investor presentation that clearly explained three key areas:

  1. Sales Performance: Detailed breakdown of growth metrics and trends
  2. Key Success Factors: What drove the 40% growth?
  3. Strategic Roadmap: How will the company sustain and scale this growth?
The professor highlighted that the analytics team had all the numbers - website visits, conversion rates, sales figures, return rates - but the data was fragmented. They had raw sales performance data but were unable to communicate the reasons for growth or the strategy to sustain it. This is a common problem many companies face: having data but lacking a coherent investor story.

The Core Problem

The analytics team had all the numbers, but the story was fragmented. Raw data alone doesn't convince investors - it needs to be woven into a compelling narrative that demonstrates:

  • Clear understanding of what worked
  • Strategic thinking about future growth
  • Confidence in the business model
  • Investment-worthy opportunities
Key Insight: Having data is not enough. Investors need to understand the "WHY" behind the numbers and the "HOW" for future growth. This requires transforming data into a strategic narrative.

2.3 Using Jasper for Narrative Creation

To transform the raw data into a compelling investor story, the team used Jasper AI, an AI-powered writing tool. The key was to provide Jasper with the right prompts that would generate investor-appropriate narratives.

Step 1: Business Tone Prompt

Prompt Strategy: Since we're creating an investor story, we need a professional, business-oriented tone that emphasizes strategic factors and measurable results.

Prompt Given to Jasper:

"Write a professional 200-word summary for an investor report explaining how UrbanThreads achieved a 40% sales growth in 2024. Emphasize digital marketing, product quality improvements, and reduced return rates."
The professor pointed out that we're giving Jasper some hints about what attributes should be used to explain the sales growth: digital marketing, product quality improvements, and reduced return rates. All these combined together led to 40% sales growth. This guidance helps Jasper create a focused narrative rather than a generic summary.

Jasper's Output (Business Tone):

"UrbanThreads achieved a remarkable 40% sales growth in 2024 through a combination of strategic marketing and enhanced product quality. By leveraging AI-driven customer segmentation, the brand reached niche audiences, improving conversion rates from 2.8% to 4.5%. At the same time, return rates dropped by 5%, signaling higher customer satisfaction and better fit prediction technology. With sustained investments in personalization and fulfillment efficiency, UrbanThreads is positioned to continue its growth trajectory into 2025."

Analyzing Jasper's Business Tone Output

Let's break down what makes this output effective:

Element What Jasper Added Why It Works
"Remarkable" Added qualifier before "40% sales growth" Creates positive emotional tone while remaining professional
"AI-driven customer segmentation" Inferred from "digital marketing" prompt Shows technological sophistication and strategic thinking
"Niche audiences" Explains HOW digital marketing worked Demonstrates targeted approach, not spray-and-pray marketing
"Fit prediction technology" Explains WHY returns decreased Links customer satisfaction to technological innovation
"Positioned to continue" Forward-looking statement Assures investors this isn't a one-time spike but sustainable growth
The professor highlighted several intelligent additions Jasper made: (1) It added "remarkable" before 40% sales growth to create impact. (2) Even though we didn't explicitly mention customer segmentation, Jasper inferred from "digital marketing" that AI-driven segmentation was used, which is why the brand could reach previously unreachable niche audiences. (3) Because the correct audience is being targeted through AI-driven marketing, the conversion improved from 2.8% to 4.5%. (4) Remember, UrbanThreads is a fashion e-commerce market, so the return rate dropping by 5% is significant because people are not returning items they purchased. (5) This drop in return rate is attributed to both higher customer satisfaction and better fit prediction technology - people are getting clothes that actually fit them well. (6) The phrase "sustained investment" subtly asks investors to continue their funding while positioning UrbanThreads for continued growth into 2025.

2.4 Using Tome for Presentation Design

Once Jasper created the narrative, the next step was to transform this text into a visually compelling presentation. This is where Tome, an AI-powered presentation tool, came into play.

How Tome Works

Tome uses AI to automatically generate presentation slides from text input. The team imported the Jasper-generated narrative using Tome's "Start from text" feature, and Tome automatically created a complete slide deck.

Input: Jasper's text narrative (4-5 sentences)
Process: Tome's AI analyzes the narrative and creates structured slides
Output: 6-slide professional presentation with auto-generated layout and visuals

While Tome's auto-generated presentation was impressive, it wasn't ready for investors immediately. The automated response from Tome provided a strong foundation, but customization was necessary to make it truly compelling.

    To humanize the data story, the team added testimonial quotes from customers. These were generated using Jasper's "testimonial tone" feature, which creates authentic-sounding customer feedback.

    Example Testimonial: "I used to return 1 out of every 3 items I ordered. Now, UrbanThreads' size recommendations are so accurate that I rarely need to return anything!" - Priya M., Bangalore

    Some slide headlines were made more investor-focused and action-oriented.

The professor highlighted that when you have user feedback or audience testimonials, you can give them to Jasper and generate better, more professional-sounding testimonials. The advantage with this approach is that you use the same color scheme for all slides, maintaining brand consistency - in this case, coral and white were used throughout.

3. Case Study 2: Healthcare Analytics - Communicating Clinical Impact to Policymakers

3.1 Context & Challenge

Organization: MedImpact Foundation
Type: Non-profit organization
Project: AI-assisted Tuberculosis (TB) screening in rural India
Problem Statement: Had compelling clinical data but needed to present it as a human-impact story, not raw statistics

The Healthcare Context

MedImpact Foundation, a non-profit organization, developed an AI algorithm to assist with tuberculosis (TB) screening in rural India by reading chest X-rays. After a year-long pilot program, they had impressive data showing significantly improved detection rates.

The professor explained that this is about AI-assisted TB screening in rural India. The challenge was that in a year-long pilot, an AI algorithm was used for reading chest X-rays. The foundation had compelling data showing improved detection rates - how an AI algorithm which can read chest X-rays could use this compelling data to show the foundation's effectiveness and how detection rates can be predicted more accurately.

What MedImpact Needed

The foundation needed to present their program's impact to the Ministry of Health, but they faced a critical communication challenge:

Word of Caution: Policymakers don't respond well to raw statistics and technical data. They need to understand the HUMAN IMPACT - lives saved, communities helped, health outcomes improved. The presentation needed to be a human-impact story to seek national expansion funding, not a technical report.

The Core Challenge

The foundation had:

  • Strong clinical data: Improved detection accuracy, faster diagnosis times, fewer missed cases
  • Field testimonials: Feedback from health workers and patients
  • Technical success: Proof that the AI algorithm worked effectively

But they needed to transform this into:

  • A human story: Not "92% accuracy" but "lives saved and suffering prevented"
  • Policy impact: Why this deserves national-scale funding
  • Emotional connection: Making policymakers care about rural health challenges
Target Audience Shift: Unlike Case Study 1 (investors who want ROI), policymakers want social impact, scalability, and evidence of transforming lives. The communication strategy must reflect this difference.

    Diagnostic accuracy improved from 78% to 92%, a 14 percentage point increase. This means the AI algorithm is correctly identifying TB cases with higher reliability than previous methods.

This is critical because in rural areas, patients might have to travel far for diagnosis and can't afford to wait days for results."

    What this means: Farmers and workers don't lose multiple days of income waiting for diagnosis; treatment begins faster, improving outcomes.

    False negatives‚Äîcases where TB is present but not detected‚Äîdecreased by 63%, from 230 to 85 cases. This is perhaps the most critical metric because false negatives mean:

    • The disease continues to progress, causing harm
    • Infected patients can unknowingly spread TB to family and community
    The professor noted: "This has resulted in a reduction in false negatives. The system ensured timely treatment for hundreds who might otherwise have been missed.     What this means: 145 more people received correct diagnoses who would have been incorrectly told they were healthy, preventing disease progression and community spread.
